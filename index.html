About

Laura Navasardian is a cellist and the William A. Loeb Fellow at the Curtis Institute of Music, where she studies with Gary Hoffman and Peter Wiley.

A native of New York City, Laura began her cello studies at age six with Vladimir Panteleev. She advanced through the Special Music School and Juilliard Pre-College before entering Curtis in 2022. Her early competition successes include first prize at the Cremona International Music Competition at age ten, the Grand Prize Virtuoso Competition in Salzburg, and semi-finalist honors at the International Tchaikovsky Competition for Young Musicians—one of the most prestigious contests in the world for emerging artists.

"Her playing is both sensitive and energetic, marked by a deep commitment to engaging her audience."

Laura has performed at Carnegie Weill Recital Hall, Merkin Concert Hall, the Museo Del Violino in Cremona, and the Saal Mozarteum in Salzburg. In 2018, she performed Tchaikovsky's Rococo Variations in Berlin under the baton of Maestro David Geringas. She counts her performance with the Boston Pops at Symphony Hall among her proudest musical moments.

At Curtis, Laura has been a regular presence in the institute's free recital series, performing chamber works by Debussy, Mendelssohn, and Bartók, and has reached wider audiences through the Curtis Recital Series summer broadcasts.
