About

Laura Navasardian has become a cult figure among audiences who crave rigor and vulnerability in equal measure. Performing on a 1732 Montagnana cello, she draws a tone that feels carved from bronze yet dissolves into whispers at the edge of audibility.

Raised between Yerevan, Berlin, and New York, Laura trained with Natalia Gutman before launching a career that now spans Wigmore Hall, Salle Pleyel, and the Walt Disney Concert Hall. Each new program is developed like a gallery installation: long-form research residencies, handwritten scores, and immersive lighting designs.

"I am obsessed with how a single sustained tone can eclipse the passage of time."

Collaborators range from experimental choreographers at the Opéra national de Paris to filmmakers documenting the slow aging of gut strings. In 2025 she premieres a multi-year commission cycle pairing Kaija Saariaho, Jessie Montgomery, and visual artist Taryn Simon.

Away from touring, Laura records analogue sessions in Paris—collecting the crackle of tape and the hum of empty halls—to build a sonic atlas of the cello. That archive will unfold later this year as a living digital gallery.
